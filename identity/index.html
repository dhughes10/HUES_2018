The Staten Island transportation system provides a setting for both mundane
and exceptional events in a culture established by commuters. Because Staten Island is disjointed from the city, its inhabitants suffer rail and ferry commutes of up to four hours; this makes interaction while traveling inevitable, and as a result of these encounters, a communal commuter memory is formed.

The project describes the relationship between the common commute and my family’s specific experience of it. My relatives embodied the commuter culture living on Staten Island, and their
anecdotes shaped my impression of the city as I grew up.

‘Identity’ is a graphic standards manual in the form of a binder. Conceptually, my identity is realized through family stories, because I see their commute as part of who I am. The dehumanizing visual design of the graphic standards manual illustrates the prosaic nature of the journey, while simultaneously masking the humanity revealed in the narratives. 

‘Routine’ is an experimental documentary in which footage of the Staten Island transportation system is overlaid with voiceovers in which my
family describe their experiences. The film frames their journeys abstractly to emphasize the drama my family draws from the ebb and flow of the commute.
